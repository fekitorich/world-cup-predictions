The analyst's notes

Morocco arrive ranked eighth in the world and carrying real momentum. They lifted the African Cup of Nations earlier this year on the back of a clean knockout run — beating Zambia, Cameroon, Nigeria and Senegal without conceding much of note — and the friendly results since have followed the same line, with 4-0 and 5-0 wins over Madagascar and Burundi either side of a 1-1 draw with Norway. A squad valued at €498.3m and built around familiar names like Bounou, Aguerd and Hakimi gives the Atlas Lions both depth and a settled spine.

The model likes them without crowning them. It makes Morocco 94.4% to clear the group stage and 34.5% to win the group outright, with a 56.7% chance of reaching the last 16. From there the odds thin in the usual way — 36.5% to make the quarter-finals, 20.3% for the semis, and 4.8% to go all the way. That champion number puts them in the second tier of contenders rather than the favourites, which feels about right for a side that does the hard defensive work well but still has to prove it over seven games.
