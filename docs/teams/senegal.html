The analyst's notes

Senegal arrive ranked 14th in the world and carrying a squad worth €478.1m, and the Lions de la Teranga's recent form looks the part. They reached the Africa Cup of Nations on a run that included shutout wins over Botswana and Benin and a 1-0 edging of Egypt, before Morocco exposed them 3-0. A 2-3 friendly loss to the USA in late May is the kind of result that tempers expectations, even if friendlies tell you less than the competitive games either side of them. Koulibaly anchors a defensive spine that kept three clean sheets across that AFCON run, and goalkeeping depth — Mendy, Diaw and Diouf — is not a worry.

The model treats them as solid rather than spectacular. They're given a 83.5% chance to clear the group stage and a 46.8% shot at the round of 16, but the odds thin quickly after that: 21.6% to reach the quarters, 9.5% for the semis, and just 1.4% to lift the trophy. Winning their group sits at 22.2%, so a runners-up route looks the likelier path through.
