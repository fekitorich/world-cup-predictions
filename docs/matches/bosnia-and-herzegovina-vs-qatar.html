The analyst's preview

Lumen Field hosts a Group B opener that looks closer on paper than the wallets suggest. Bosnia carry a squad worth €151.6m against Qatar's €19.93m, and their recent form reads steadier — draws with Italy and Wales in European qualifying, a 3-1 over Romania — while Qatar arrive on a thin run that includes a 0-3 Arab Cup loss to Tunisia and home defeats to Palestine and Zimbabwe. The model frames it as a modest home edge: 1.35 expected goals to 1.07, Bosnia favoured at 42.5% with the draw (28.5%) and a Qatar win (29.0%) almost level behind. This is a low-scoring lean, with under 2.5 the more likely side at 56.5% and 1-1 the single most probable score at 13.5%.

Here is the genuine split. Polymarket prices Bosnia at 58.5 cents and shoves Qatar down to just 16.5 — far more lopsided than the model, which gives Qatar nearly thirteen points more. The market effectively reads this as a comfortable home win; the model reads it as an open game where a draw or a Qatar result is well within range. On totals the two broadly agree — over 2.5 at 43.5% model versus 47 cents, BTTS near 49% versus 46.5 — so the real argument is purely about how dead Qatar's chances are.
