The analyst's preview

Belgium arrive as clear favourites, and the shape of the matchup explains why. The ninth-ranked side carry a squad valued at €547.5m against Egypt's €116.48m, and they come in scorching, beating Tunisia 5-0 and Croatia 2-0 in their last two friendlies. The model reads it as control rather than a procession: 1.5 expected goals for Belgium against 0.71 for Egypt, a 55.7% win probability, and a healthy 28.3% chance of the draw. Egypt are no pushover defensively — three clean sheets in their last ten, including a goalless draw away to Spain — and that shows in a modest 39.9% both-teams-to-score figure and a most-likely scoreline of just 1-0 at 16.0%.

The interesting friction is with the market. Polymarket prices Belgium at 61.5 cents to win, noticeably bolder than the model's 55.7%, and it expects a more open game: over 2.5 goals trades at 49.5 cents against the model's 37.8%, with both-teams-to-score at 48.5 versus 39.9%. So the disagreement is real and worth flagging — the market backs Belgium harder and bets on goals, while the model sees a tighter, lower-scoring win. Egypt's resilience is the genuine open question between the two views.
