The analyst's preview

Portugal arrive in Miami as the heavier name — fifth in the FIFA ranking to Colombia's 13th, and a squad valued at just over a billion euros against Colombia's €302m — and the model reads the matchup that way without overcommitting. It gives Portugal a 1.36 expected-goals edge to Colombia's 1.05 and makes them favourites at 43.2%, with the draw and a Colombia win almost level behind at 28.5% and 28.4%. This is a close, low-scoring profile: over 2.5 goals lands at just 43.3%, the most likely scoreline is a 1-1 at 13.5%, and Portugal are marginally the side more likely to open it, scoring first 51% of the time. Both teams arrive in form, Colombia unbeaten in their last four friendlies and Portugal beaten only once in their last ten.

The market broadly agrees but leans a touch harder on Portugal, pricing the away win at 46.5 cents against the model's 43.2% — a real if modest gap that frames this as a live question rather than a settled one. The clearer disagreement is on goals at both ends: Polymarket has both teams to score at 53 cents, comfortably above the model's 49%, so the market expects a slightly more open game than the numbers do.
