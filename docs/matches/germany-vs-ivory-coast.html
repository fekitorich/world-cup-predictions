The analyst's preview

Germany arrive in Toronto on a near-flawless run, nine wins in their last ten with the only blemish a 0-2 loss in Slovakia, and a squad valued at €947m against Ivory Coast's €522m. But the gap is narrower than the FIFA rankings (10th versus 34th) suggest: the Ivorians have won eight of ten themselves, including a 2-1 away win over France this June. The model reads it as a clear but far from settled favourite's game, projecting 1.42 expected goals for Germany against 0.97 — enough for a 46.7% win probability, with the draw live at 28.1% and Ivory Coast holding a real 25.2% puncher's chance. The likeliest scorelines are tight: 1-1 at 13.3%, 1-0 at 12.4%, and a goalless draw still 9.8%.

This is where the model and the market part company sharply. Polymarket prices Germany at 63.5 cents, treating the win as close to a formality, while the model's 46.7% says it is anything but. The market also leans higher-scoring, pricing over 2.5 goals at 52.5 cents against the model's 42.8%. The two only truly agree on both teams scoring, near-identical at 48 cents and 47.7%. Treat Germany's margin, not the result, as the genuine open question.
