The analyst's preview

Japan come into Dallas as the model's clear favourite, and the form lines explain why. Despite Sweden carrying the more valuable squad on paper — €406m to Japan's €271m — it's Japan riding three straight 1-0 wins over Iceland, England and Scotland, while Sweden have lost five of their last ten and stumbled through European qualifying. The model reads it as a controlled home edge: 1.9 expected goals to 0.84, a 61.95% win probability, and a 64.6% chance of striking first. The likeliest scorelines are tight and Japanese — 1-0 at 11.8%, 2-0 at 11.7% — with both teams scoring rated just under a coin flip at 48.83%.

This is where the market pushes back hard. Polymarket prices Japan at only 47 cents to the model's 62%, and lifts Sweden to 26.5 cents against the model's 15.39% — a wide gap that treats this as far more of an open contest than the numbers suggest. The draw is nudged up too, 27.5 cents versus 22.66%. On totals the two broadly agree, with over 2.5 goals near even (51.68% model, 45.5 cents market). The honest read: the model loves Japan's recent run, the market trusts it noticeably less, and that disagreement is the bet worth watching.
