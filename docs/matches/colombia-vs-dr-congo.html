The analyst's preview

Colombia carry the favourite's profile into Guadalajara: ranked 13th to DR Congo's 46th, a squad valued at €302m against €144m, and a recent run that includes a 6-3 qualifying win over Venezuela and a 4-0 away win at Mexico. The model reads it as a controlled home job rather than a rout, projecting 1.41 expected goals to DR Congo's 0.55 and pricing Colombia at 57.6% to win, with the draw a live 28.5%. That low away xG reflects a Congo side that has been hard to break down — a goalless draw with Denmark, clean sheets against Cameroon and Benin — and the scorelines bear it out: 1-0 is the single most likely result at 19.4%, with 0-0 next at 14.7%.

The market is more emphatic. Polymarket has Colombia at 66.5 cents, nearly nine points clear of the model, and trims the draw to 21.5%, so this is a genuine disagreement about how comfortable the favourite really is. The two sides also split on goals: the model sees just 31.1% for over 2.5, while the market sits at 43 cents, and both-teams-to-score is priced at 38.5 against the model's 32.5%. If you trust the numbers, the value is in a tighter, lower-scoring game than the betting suggests.
