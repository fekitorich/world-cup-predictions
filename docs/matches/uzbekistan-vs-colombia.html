The analyst's preview

That translates to a 57.98% win probability for Colombia, 27.11% for the draw and just 14.91% for Uzbekistan. The shape is low-scoring rather than a rout: under 2.5 goals is the likelier outcome at 64%, both teams scoring is only a 36.88% shot, and the model's four most probable scorelines are all tight — 0-1, 0-2, 0-0 and 1-1. Where the model and market part company is on degree. Polymarket prices Colombia at 70.5 cents to win, well clear of the model's 58%, and trims Uzbekistan to 9.5 and the draw to 20.5. The market also expects more goals, pricing over 2.5 at 49 cents against the model's 35.95%. So the disagreement isn't about who wins — it's whether Colombia win comfortably and openly, or grind through a cagey, tight afternoon. Treat that as genuinely open.
