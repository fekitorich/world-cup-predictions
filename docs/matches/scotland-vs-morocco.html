The analyst's preview

Morocco arrive in Boston as the clear class side, ranked 8th in the world against Scotland's 43rd, with a squad valued at €498m to €170m. The model reflects that gap bluntly: Morocco are 65.8% to win, Scotland just 10.1%, with the draw at 24.1%. The expected goals tell the same story, 1.67 to 0.50, and Morocco's recent form backs it up — they reached this point on the back of an unbeaten Africa Cup of Nations run, beating Senegal 3-0 and shutting out Nigeria. Scotland's 4-0 friendly win over Bolivia flatters a team that lost to both Japan and Ivory Coast in March. The model's single likeliest outcome is a 0-1 Morocco win at 18.6%, with Morocco favoured to score first 67.8% of the time.

Where it gets interesting is the disagreement with the market. Polymarket prices Scotland far more generously at 22.5 cents, more than double the model's read, and trims Morocco to 49.5. The market also leans higher-scoring: both teams to score sits at 47.5 cents versus the model's 32.4%, and over 2.5 goals at 40.5 against 36.9%. So the shape is agreed — Morocco on top — but how comfortably, and how open, is a genuine open question.
