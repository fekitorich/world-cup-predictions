The analyst's preview

The shape here is a clear favourite against a side arriving in poor form. The Netherlands sit seventh in the world to Sweden's 38th, with a squad valued at €754.2m against €406.1m, and the model leans heavily on that gap: it projects 2.1 expected goals for the hosts to just 0.96 for Sweden, and makes the Dutch 63.1% to win in Houston. Form backs the read. The Netherlands lost only once in their last ten and battered Finland, Malta and Lithuania 4-0 in qualifying, while Sweden have collapsed badly, taking only a couple of recent wins amid heavy defeats to Switzerland (1-4 and 0-2) and Kosovo. The model has the Netherlands 65.1% to score first and 2-0 as the single likeliest scoreline at 10.3%.

The market broadly agrees but trims the edges. Polymarket prices the Dutch at 59.5 cents to the model's 63.1%, nudging the draw and Sweden slightly longer in the model's eyes. The sharper disagreement is on goals: the model sees Over 2.5 at 59% against the market's 51.5 cents, and both-teams-to-score at 54.6% versus 50.5. So the genuine open question isn't who wins but how open it gets — the model expects more goals than the market is willing to pay for.
