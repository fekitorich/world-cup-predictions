The analyst's review

Ivory Coast got the win their underdog status didn't predict, edging Ecuador 1-0 in Philadelphia to claim all three points in this group E opener. The single goal was enough against an Ecuador side that had drawn five of its previous ten and arrived ranked eleven places higher at 23rd in the world.

This one goes down as a miss for both the model and the market. The model leaned away from an Ivory Coast win entirely, pricing them at just 26.97% with the draw the most likely single outcome at 37.57%, and its locked bracket pick of a 0-0 with Ecuador edging it on aggregate value was wrong on both score and result. Polymarket was no closer, making Ecuador clear favourites at 39.5 cents against Ivory Coast's 26.5. The lone consolation is that the exact 1-0 scoreline was the model's third-likeliest result at 14.41%, behind only 0-0 and 0-1, so the low-scoring shape was well read even as the winner wasn't. Days like this are the reminder that a 26.97% chance still comes in better than one time in four.

The analyst's preview

This is a low-scoring chess match by both readings, but the model leans harder into that than the market does. Ecuador's defensive solidity — clean sheets against Canada, Paraguay and a 1-0 win over Argentina dot their recent form — pairs with an Ivory Coast attack the model rates at just 0.69 expected goals to 0.83 for the visitors. That edge tips Ecuador to a 35.5% win against the Ivorians' 27%, but the model's real conviction is the draw at 37.6%, and the single most likely result is a goalless 0-0 at 22.5%. Ecuador are favoured to score first (42.5%) and the contest is expected to be cagey from the start, with a level half-time the runaway favourite at 57.3%.

Where model and market part ways is the goals total. The model sees under 2.5 as comfortably the bet, pricing over 2.5 at just 19.6% and both teams to score at 28.7%. Polymarket is far more open on both, marking over 2.5 at 32.5 cents and BTTS at 41.5 cents — a meaningful gap that says the market expects a livelier game than the numbers do. The moneyline is closer to agreement, with Polymarket at 37.5 away, 32.5 draw and 29.5 home. Treat the goals question as genuinely open.
