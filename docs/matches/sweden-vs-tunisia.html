The analyst's review

Sweden buried Tunisia 5-1 in Monterrey, a result that lands at the very edge of what anyone had drawn up. The model gave over 4.5 goals a 7.1 percent chance and over 5.5 just 2.4 percent, so six goals in a single game was firmly in the tail. Sweden's expected goals of 1.22 became five on the night, while Tunisia's 0.96 at least produced a consolation. After a run that included a 0-5 loss to Belgium and a 0-1 to Austria, Tunisia's defensive frailty showed up again here.

On the call itself, both views had Sweden as favourites and both were right about the winner: the model priced the home side at 41.4 percent, Polymarket more bullish at 50.5 cents, and the locked bracket pick of a 1-1 home result counts as a hit on result if not on margin. Where everyone missed badly was the scale. Nobody saw a five-goal Swedish haul coming, with a draw rated 30.2 percent and the most likely scoreline a humble 1-1 at 13.9 percent. Right team, wildly understated emphasis.
