The analyst's preview

France open Group I as clear favourites against Senegal, and the numbers behind that are straightforward: top-ranked, a squad valued at €1.52bn against Senegal's €478m, and an attack the model expects to generate 1.42 goals to 0.86. That edge shows up across the board — France are 55.6% to score first, the most likely scoreline is 1-0 at 13.9%, and a low-event game is in play, with over 2.5 goals only a 39.8% shot and both teams scoring rated 44.3%. Senegal arrive in decent form themselves, with an Africa Cup of Nations run that included wins over Egypt and Mali, but the model frames this as France controlling without running away with it.

The interesting gap is on the moneyline. Our model makes France 49.6% to win, with the draw a live 28.3% and Senegal 22%; Polymarket is far bolder, pricing France at 66.5c and shrinking Senegal to just 12.5c. That is a wide disagreement — the market sees something close to a routine French win, while the model treats this as a genuine contest where one Senegal goal changes the texture. On totals the two are closer, though the market still leans higher, pricing over 2.5 at 50.5c against our 39.8%.
