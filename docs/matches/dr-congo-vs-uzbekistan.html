The analyst's preview

Two evenly matched debutant-tier sides meet in Atlanta, and the model sees a tight, low-event game. The expected goals tell the story: 0.73 for DR Congo, 0.78 for Uzbekistan, virtually nothing between them and not much for either. That pushes the draw to the front of the moneyline at 37.9 percent, ahead of Uzbekistan at 32.5 and DR Congo at 29.5, despite the Congolese carrying the higher FIFA ranking (46 to 50) and the heavier squad, valued at €143.9m against €85.3m. This shapes up as a goalkeeping afternoon: a 0-0 is the single most likely scoreline at 22.8 percent, both teams to score sits at just 28.6 percent, and the model gives only a 19.2 percent chance of more than two and a half goals.

That last number is where the market pushes back hardest. Polymarket prices over 2.5 goals at 40.5 cents, more than double the model's read, and both-teams-to-score at 47.5 against the model's 28.6. The market also leans firmly toward DR Congo at 41 cents, where the model has a coin-flippier three-way. So the genuine disagreement is twofold: the market expects more goals and more from the Congolese. Treat both as open questions rather than settled facts.
