The analyst's preview

Spain arrive ranked second in the world with a squad valued at €1.22bn, a gulf over Cape Verde's €54.5m and the 67-place ranking gap that frames this one as a straightforward mismatch. The model agrees: it projects 2.98 expected goals for Spain against just 0.41, and makes them 88.1% to win with the draw a distant 9.3% and a Cape Verde upset barely registering at 2.7%. Spain are 84.9% to open the scoring and 67.2% to lead at the break, and the most likely scorelines cluster around 2-0, 3-0 and 4-0. None of that should surprise after a qualifying run that included 6-0 and 4-0 results, though recent friendlies — a 1-1 with Iraq, a goalless draw with Egypt — were scratchier.

The market backs the favourite even harder, pricing Spain at 91.5 cents to the model's 88.1, and it leans into the goals too: Polymarket has over 2.5 at 72.5 cents where the model sits at 65.7%, and over 3.5 essentially a coin flip at 50.5 against the model's 43.8%. The honest read is that both see the same result; the open question is purely the margin, where the model is a shade more cautious about a blowout.
