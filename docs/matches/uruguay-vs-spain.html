The analyst's preview

This is a heavyweight against a grinder. Spain arrive ranked second in the world, riding a long unbeaten run built on 4-0 and 6-0 qualifying scorelines, with a squad valued at €1.22bn against Uruguay's €359m. The model reflects that gap without treating it as a foregone conclusion: it gives Spain 1.37 expected goals to Uruguay's 0.7 and makes them 52.3% to win, with the draw a live 29.1% and Uruguay just 18.6%. The shape it expects is a controlled Spanish edge rather than a rout — Uruguay's recent form is a wall of clean sheets and goalless draws (Algeria, Mexico, Chile), and the model leans that way too, with 0-1, 0-0 and 1-1 its three likeliest scores and a 13.2% chance nobody scores at all.

The interesting friction is in the goals markets. The model sees a tight, low-scoring game: over 2.5 at just 34.4% and both teams to score at 38.3%. Polymarket disagrees sharply, pricing over 2.5 at 49 cents and BTTS at 51.5 — essentially coin flips. The market also nudges Spain's win price up to 59.5 against the model's 52.3. So treat the winner as a genuine open question, but note the clear split on how open the game itself will be.
