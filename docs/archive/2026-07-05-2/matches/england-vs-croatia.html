The analyst's preview

England arrive in Dallas as the heavy favourite, and the model leaves little doubt why. A squad valued at €1,360m against Croatia's €387.3m, a number four world ranking against eleven, and a recent run that includes back-to-back clean sheets over Serbia all push expected goals to 1.58 for England and just 0.69 for Croatia. That gap drives a 58.3 percent home win, with the draw at 26.2 percent and Croatia given only 15.5 percent. England are favoured to open the scoring 62 percent of the time, and the most likely scorelines are tellingly cagey — 1-0 at 15.8 percent, 2-0 at 12.9 — pointing to control rather than a goal rush.

This is a low-event projection: over 2.5 goals lands at just 39.6 percent and both teams to score at 40.1 percent, consistent with a side that wins by suffocation. The market broadly agrees on the favourite but trims the edges, pricing England at 56.5 cents and giving Croatia a slightly warmer 18.5 against the model's 15.5. The clearer disagreement is on goals: Polymarket has both teams to score at 47.5 cents and over 2.5 at 44.5, noticeably above the model. Whether Croatia's attack can puncture England's defence is the open question here.
