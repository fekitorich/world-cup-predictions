The analyst's preview

Argentina arrive ranked third in the world with a squad valued at €782.5m against Austria's €242.2m, and the model treats this as exactly the mismatch the numbers suggest. It hands the hosts a 1.88 to 0.51 expected-goals edge and a 70.1% win probability, with Austria down at 8.8% and the draw at 21.2%. Argentina are favoured to open the scoring 71.1% of the time, and the most likely outcomes are all clean sheets — 1-0 at 16.8%, 2-0 at 16.2%, 3-0 at 10.2% — so this projects as a controlled, low-event win rather than a shootout. Tellingly, both teams to score sits at just 34.3%, reflecting how little the model expects from an Austrian side that has nonetheless quietly won eight of its last ten.

The market is more cautious about the favourite. Polymarket prices Argentina at 61.5 cents against the model's 70%, and gives Austria nearly double the model's chance, 16.5 cents to 8.8%. The same split shows on both-teams-to-score, 47.5 cents versus 34.3%, suggesting traders rate Austria's defensive names and recent form more highly than the goals model does. The totals barely differ — over 2.5 sits at 42.7% to the market's 48% — so the genuine open question is simply how much resistance Austria can muster.
