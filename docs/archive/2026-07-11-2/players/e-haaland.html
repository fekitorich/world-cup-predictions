The analyst's notes

Haaland's club output reads like a metronome: 22 Premier League goals in 32 appearances across 2024, then 27 in 35 across 2025, the latter sharpened by eight assists rather than just finishing. The Champions League tells the same story twice — eight goals in each campaign from roughly 760 minutes — so the elite-defence test does nothing to slow him. That consistency at the highest club level is the strongest argument for treating him as a tournament-defining centre-forward.

The international ledger is even more emphatic when the chances come: 16 goals in eight World Cup qualifiers and seven in six Nations League games during 2024, a strike rate Norway lives off. The caution is recency — two goals in two 2026 qualifiers but a blank in two friendlies (6.5 rating). Norway sit 31st in FIFA's ranking, so service, not the finisher, is the variable. Give Haaland the volume of chances his record demands and the goals follow; starve him and the supporting cast has to deliver.
