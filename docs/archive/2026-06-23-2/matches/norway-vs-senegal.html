The analyst's preview

This is the matchup the model can't separate. Senegal arrive ranked 14th in the world to Norway's 31st, and the expected goals back that edge ever so slightly: 1.21 for Senegal against 1.13 for Norway, a margin small enough that the moneyline splits almost evenly — 37.2 percent for Senegal, 33.4 for Norway, with a thick 29.4 percent draw reflecting how tight this projects. Norway come in on a strong run, including 4-1 wins over Italy and Estonia in qualifying, while Senegal's recent record is more mixed, an AFCON campaign followed by a 3-2 friendly loss to the USA. The model reads this as a coin-flip leaning marginally to the more highly ranked side, with low scoring: only 41 percent of outcomes clear 2.5 goals, and 1-1 tops the scoreline chart at 13.8 percent.

The market sees it very differently, and the gap is the story. Polymarket prices Norway as clear favourites at 45 cents, with Senegal down at just 28.5 — almost the mirror image of the model, which makes Senegal the slight pick. The market also expects more goals, pricing both teams to score at 55.5 percent against the model's 48.2. That's a genuine open question worth watching, not a settled one.
