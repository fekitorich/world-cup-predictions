The analyst's notes

The Elephants arrive in form. Champions of Africa for the third time in 2023, Ivory Coast have turned that momentum into a friendly run that catches the eye: a 4-0 win over South Korea, a 1-0 win at Scotland, and most strikingly a 2-1 victory away to France in June. That is the kind of result that travels well into a World Cup, the fourth qualification in the nation's history and the first since 2014. A squad valued at €522.1m and ranked 34th in the world is built on a deep, well-stocked defence, from Kossounou and Diomande to the Doué and Konan names familiar across Europe.

The model is respectful rather than reverent. It makes Ivory Coast comfortable to clear the round of 32 at 85.9% and roughly a coin-flip to reach the last 16 at 41.6%, with a 22.4% chance of winning the group outright. Beyond that the odds thin quickly: 16.4% to reach the quarter-finals and just 0.7% to lift the trophy. A January loss to Egypt at AFCON is the reminder that the ceiling and the floor still sit some distance apart.
