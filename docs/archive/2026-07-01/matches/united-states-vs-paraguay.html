The shape of the matchup explains the gap — a squad valued at €385.65m against Paraguay's €153.65m, a side ranked 16th hosting one ranked 40th, and the memory of a 2-1 US win when these two met as recently as last November. The most likely scoreline is a tight 1-0 at 13.7%, with 1-1 close behind at 12.4%, so this reads as a controlled home win rather than a rout: the US are 59.4% to score first but only 43% of games here clear 2.5 goals.

Where it gets interesting is the moneyline. The market is more cautious about the hosts, pricing them at 47.5 cents against the model's 54.6%, and crediting Paraguay at 24.5 cents versus the model's 18.9%. That is a genuine disagreement worth respecting — the US friendly form is patchy, with losses to Germany, Portugal and Belgium among recent home games. On totals and both teams to score the two sides essentially agree, so the open question is purely how much resistance Paraguay offer.
