The analyst's preview

This is a mismatch dressed up as a fixture. Germany, ranked 10th and carrying a €947m squad, arrive on the back of seven straight wins, against a Curaçao side ranked 82nd whose €25.78m squad has shipped four to Scotland and five to Australia in recent friendlies. The model reads it accordingly: 3.5 expected goals for Germany against just 0.55, a 89.95% home win, and an 84.78% chance Germany open the scoring. The likeliest outcomes are all comfortable home margins — 3-0 at 12.45%, 4-0 at 10.9%, 2-0 at 10.67% — and over 2.5 goals lands at 76.93%. The shape is straightforward: territory, possession and pressure flowing one way.

Where the model and market part company is in degree, not direction. Polymarket prices Germany even shorter at 94 cents to the model's 90, and leans harder into goals, putting over 2.5 at 82.5% against the model's 77%. The sharper split is at the other end: the model gives Curaçao a 41.2% chance of scoring at some point, where the market sees only 32.5%. So both agree Germany should win at a canter — the open question is whether Curaçao manage to register on the scoreboard at all.
