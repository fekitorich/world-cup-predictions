The analyst's preview

France arrive as the world's top-ranked side and the model treats this as their game to lose, pricing them at 54.2% to win against 20.6% for Norway and a 25.2% draw. The expected-goals split tells the same story, 1.69 to 0.96, and France are favoured to open the scoring at 59.1%. None of that makes Norway harmless: a squad worth €589.9m has won six of its last ten and put four past Italy in qualifying, and the model still gives them a one-in-five shout. But the shape here is a stronger, deeper France — squad value €1.52bn, ranked first to Norway's 31st — probing a side that may spend long spells defending. A 1-1 is the single likeliest score at 12%, with 0-1 and 0-2 close behind.

The market and the model are reading this almost identically, which is its own kind of signal. Polymarket has France a fraction shorter on the moneyline at 52.5 cents and Norway slightly longer at 22.5, the only real nudge being marginally more faith in the draw. Totals are a near-perfect match — over 2.5 sits at 49.4% on the model and 49 cents on the market — and both lean to roughly coin-flip both-teams-to-score, 50.8% against 53.5 cents. No edge worth chasing; just agreement.
