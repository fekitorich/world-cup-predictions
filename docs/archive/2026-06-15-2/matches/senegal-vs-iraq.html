The analyst's preview

Senegal arrive as heavy favourites and the numbers explain why. The model hands them a 1.79 to 0.47 expected-goals edge, the kind of gap you get when a side ranked 14th with a squad valued at €478.1m meets one ranked 57th worth €21.2m. That translates to a 69.3% win probability against just 8.6% for Iraq, with the draw at 22.1%. The shape is a control-and-suffocate job: Senegal are 70.6% to score first, and the likeliest scorelines are all clean sheets — 1-0 at 18.2%, 2-0 at 16.7%, then 3-0 at 10.0%. Iraq's resilience is real, though; they held Spain 1-1 and Saudi Arabia 0-0 recently, and a 0-0 here carries 10.9%.

On the headline price there is essentially no argument: Polymarket has Senegal at 69.5c, almost exactly the model's read. The disagreement lives in the goals markets. The model sees this as a tighter, lower-scoring game than the market does — over 2.5 goals at 39.3% against 48.5c, and both teams to score at just 31.7% versus a punchy 45c. If you trust Iraq's defensive record, the model's caution looks well placed; if Senegal's firepower runs free, the market's appetite for goals will be vindicated. That is the genuine open question.
