The analyst's notes

Spain arrive ranked second in the world with a squad valued at €1.22bn, and the form to match the billing. Their World Cup qualifying campaign was close to ruthless — a 6-0 away win over Türkiye, four-goal hauls against Georgia and Bulgaria, and only a 2-2 home draw with Türkiye to blot the record. The friendlies since have been flatter: a goalless stalemate with Egypt and a 1-1 draw with Iraq sit alongside a 3-0 dismissal of Serbia and a 3-1 win in Peru. The attacking ceiling is obvious; the question is whether the recent draws are noise or a sign of a side coasting between competitive fixtures.

The model treats Spain as genuine contenders rather than favourites. They are given a 75% chance of winning their group and a near-certain 99.2% to clear the round of 32, but the path narrows quickly: 37.6% to reach the semi-finals, 24.5% to make the final, and 14.4% to lift the trophy. That last figure makes them one of the strongest picks in the field without being a runaway, a reflection of both their quality and the depth of competition ahead.
