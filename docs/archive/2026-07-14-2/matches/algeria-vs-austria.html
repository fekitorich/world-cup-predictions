The analyst's preview

Algeria get the nominal home billing in Kansas City and the model leans their way, projecting 1.33 expected goals to Austria's 1.02 and pricing the hosts at 43% to win against 28% for Austria, with 29% draws. The case is built on form: Algeria beat the Netherlands 1-0 in June and ran deep into the Africa Cup of Nations, while their ranking (28th) sits just behind Austria's (24th) and squad values are near-level at €256.9m to €242.2m. This is a tight, low-volume game in the model's eyes — over 2.5 goals lands at just 42%, both teams to score at 48%, and the single likeliest outcome is a 1-1 draw at 14%, with 1-0 and 0-0 close behind.

The disagreement here is stark and worth flagging. Polymarket has it the other way round, making Austria the favourite at 44.5 cents and Algeria the outsider at just 25.5 — almost a mirror image of the model's read. Austria's qualifying run, including a 5-1 friendly win over Ghana and a settled defence around Alaba and Danso, is clearly weighing on the market. On the goals markets the two broadly agree — over 2.5 within a point either side — so the real question is purely who wins, and on that the model and the money genuinely diverge.
