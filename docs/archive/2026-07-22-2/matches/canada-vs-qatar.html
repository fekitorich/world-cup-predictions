The analyst's preview

This is a heavy mismatch on paper, and the model reads it that way. Canada, ranked 30th to Qatar's 55th and carrying a squad valued near ten times higher (€198.65m to €19.93m), get an expected 3.18 goals at BC Place against just 0.61. That feeds an 86.3% win probability, with the draw a distant 9.8% and a Qatar upset priced at under 4%. The shape fits both teams' recent form: Qatar arrive on a grim run — a 0-3 loss to Tunisia, defeats to Palestine and Zimbabwe — while Canada are unbeaten across their last five friendlies, if often by narrow margins. The model leans on a fast Canadian start too, giving them a 66% lead at the break and an 82% chance of scoring first. The likeliest scorelines cluster around 3-0, 2-0 and 4-0.

Where the numbers part ways is on degree, not direction. Polymarket has Canada at 74.5 cents, a clear step below the model's 86%, and inflates the draw to 17.5 against the model's 9.8%. The gap widens on goals: the model makes over 2.5 a 73% shot, the market only 48.5 cents. So both sides agree Canada should win — the open question is whether they win as emphatically as the model insists.
