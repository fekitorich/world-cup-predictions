The analyst's preview

This is the closest thing to a coin flip Group G offers. The model gives Egypt 34.5%, the draw 32.6% and Iran 32.95%, with projected goals almost identical at 0.99 to 0.96 — a tight, low-event game between sides that defend better than they create. That low ceiling runs through the totals: over 2.5 lands at just 31.2%, both teams to score at 39.7%, and the single most likely scoreline is 0-0 at 14.8%, narrowly ahead of 1-1. Egypt's slim edge rests partly on a squad valued at €116m against Iran's €32m, though Iran sit eight places higher in the FIFA ranking at 21st, which keeps the matchup honest.

The disagreement is on Egypt's price. Polymarket has them at 43.5 cents to win, against the model's 34.5%, and shades Iran out to 26.5 where the model holds 33%. So the market sees Egypt as clear favourites and the model sees three roughly equal outcomes — a genuine open question rather than evidence either way. The market also leans slightly more towards goals, pricing over 2.5 at 35.5 and both teams scoring at 44.5, both above the model. If you trust the numbers, the value is on Iran's chances and on the game staying tight.
