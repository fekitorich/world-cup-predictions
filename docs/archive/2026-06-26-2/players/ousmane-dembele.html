The analyst's notes

Dembélé arrives as the attacking spearhead of the world's top-ranked side, and his club numbers explain why. The 2024 campaign was the peak: 21 goals and seven assists in 30 Ligue 1 games at a 7.72 rating, plus eight goals and six assists across 15 Champions League outings. The 2025 follow-up cooled slightly without falling off — 10 league goals in 22 starts and another eight in Europe, though his minutes nearly halved, hinting at a more rotated role.

The caveat is France itself. That positional shuffle is the thing to watch. A player producing a goal involvement roughly every other club match should be a genuine tournament threat, but only if France play him where he scores rather than where he tracks back.
