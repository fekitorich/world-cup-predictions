The analyst's preview

This is a clear mismatch on paper, and the model treats it as one. Uruguay sit 17th in the world to Saudi Arabia's 61st, and the gulf in squad value — €359.3m against €40.68m — is even starker. The Dixon-Coles numbers follow suit: 1.57 expected goals for Uruguay, just 0.43 for the Saudis, with the South Americans favoured at 65.6% and Saudi Arabia given only a 9.3% chance of the win. Uruguay are also overwhelmingly likely to open the scoring (67.7%), and the model's most probable outcomes — 0-1 at 20.8% and 0-2 at 16.7% — paint the picture of a controlled away win rather than a goal-fest. A 0-0 still rates 14%, reflecting Uruguay's recent run of blanks against Algeria, Mexico and Chile.

Market and model agree closely on the result — Polymarket has Uruguay at 66.5 cents, essentially level with the model — so the genuine disagreement is on goals. The model sees this as a low-scoring affair, pricing over 2.5 at 32.4% and both teams to score at just 28.1%. The market is markedly more open, paying 45.5 cents on over 2.5 and 41.5 on BTTS. If you trust the model's read on Saudi Arabia's shot-shy attack, the unders and the no-BTTS look like the value.
