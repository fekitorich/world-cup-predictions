The analyst's review

Scotland edged Haiti 1-0 at Gillette Stadium, a tight, low-scoring afternoon that gave the favourites their three points without ever turning into the comfortable win their squad value and ranking might suggest. A single goal settled it, the kind of margin that fit a fixture both the model and the market read as Scotland's to lose but not by much.

On the call, this was a solid night. The model made Scotland the pick at 45.9% to win, against 28.1% for Haiti and 26.1% for the draw, and the away result duly landed — and the locked bracket pick had the right winner even if the scoreline was off. Polymarket leaned harder still, pricing Scotland at 62 cents, and that confidence was rewarded. Where the numbers missed was on goals: the model gave the over 2.5 a coin-flip 52.2% and both teams to score 55.7%, yet a clean sheet and a single strike beat both. The exact 0-1 was the second-likeliest scoreline at 9.4%, so even the surprise wasn't really one. A good day for the lean, a quieter one for the goal count.

The analyst's preview

Scotland arrive as favourites and they look it on paper: ranked 43rd to Haiti's 83rd, with a squad valued at €170.25m against €55.9m, and both sides land in Boston in good touch after 4-0 wins in early June. The model leans Scotland's way without committing to it, projecting 1.58 expected goals to Haiti's 1.19 and pricing the away win at 45.87%, the draw at 26.08% and a Haiti win at 28.05%. That is a clear edge but a long way from a formality, and the scoreline spread reflects it — 1-1 tops the chart at 12.38%, with 0-1 and 1-2 close behind, the picture of a tight game Scotland are expected to nudge rather than dominate.

This is where the market parts company with us. Polymarket has Scotland at 62 cents, a full sixteen points clearer than the model's 45.87%, and trims Haiti to just 16 cents against our 28.05%. The two broadly agree on goals — over 2.5 sits at 52.23% to the market's 57.5%, and both-teams-to-score near the coin-flip at 55.74% versus 53.5% — so the disagreement is purely about how safe Scotland's win is. The model says live underdog; the market says comfortable. Treat that gap as the genuine open question here.
