The analyst's preview

Mexico get this group A opener at home in Guadalajara, and the model treats them as clear but not overwhelming favourites: 1.53 expected goals to South Korea's 0.97, which translates to a 49.7% win probability against 23.4% for the visitors and a 26.9% draw. The shape is a familiar one — a higher-ranked host (15th to South Korea's 25th) leaning on home advantage and the better recent run, including a 5-1 win over Serbia this month, while Korea arrive with a streakier profile and the reminder that they held Mexico 2-2 away just last September. Mexico are favoured to open the scoring at 55.8%, and the most likely scoreline is a tight 1-1 at 12.8%, just ahead of 1-0.

On the moneyline the model and Polymarket are essentially holding hands — 49.7% versus 48.5% on Mexico, the draw and away prices a fraction apart — so there's no edge to chase on the result. The interesting gap is on goals. The model sees a livelier game: 71.9% for over 1.5 against the market's 65.5%, and 45.7% for over 2.5 where Polymarket sits at 40%. Both-teams-to-score is close at 49.3% to 46.5%. If you believe the model, the value is in the over.
