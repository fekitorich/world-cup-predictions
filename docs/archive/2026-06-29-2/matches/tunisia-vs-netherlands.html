The analyst's preview

The shape here is a heavy mismatch. The Netherlands arrive ranked 7th against a Tunisia side 44th in the world, and the squad valuations tell the same story: €754m against €69.95m. The model reads it as a clear away job, projecting 1.77 expected goals for the Dutch to Tunisia's 0.64 and pricing the Netherlands at 64% to win, with the draw at 23% and a home win down at just 12%. That's built on form too — the Dutch have lost only twice in their last ten, while Tunisia were beaten 5-0 by Belgium and 1-0 by Austria in their most recent friendlies. The most likely scorelines are all low and Dutch-favoured: 0-1 at 15% and 0-2 at 14%.

On the result, model and market are essentially in lockstep — Polymarket has the Netherlands at 65.5 cents, the draw at 22.5 and Tunisia at 13.5, almost identical to the model. The disagreement is about goals. The market sees this as a coin-flip for over 2.5 (50 cents) where the model has it at 43%, and prices both teams to score at 48.5 cents against the model's 40%. So the open question isn't who wins, but whether Tunisia find the net at all.
